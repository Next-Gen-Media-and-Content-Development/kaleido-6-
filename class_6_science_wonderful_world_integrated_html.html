1.1 What Is Science?

As human beings, we have always been curious about our surroundings. We explore and ask questions from childhood. Science continues this fascinating journey by helping us understand the beautiful world we live in.

Science is a way of thinking, observing and doing things to understand the world we live in and to uncover the secrets of the universe. It is like a big adventure where we ask questions, explore, and try to understand how things work.

Science is like a giant, unending jigsaw puzzle. Every discovery adds a new piece. Sometimes an old piece is moved when better evidence is found. Science updates itself!
⛰️

Mountains & Oceans

Science explains how landforms form and what lives in different habitats.

🌙

Stars & Space

Science helps us ask why stars shine and how far away they are.

🌸

Plants & Animals

Seeds, flowers, butterflies and forests all invite scientific questions.

To be a wise person, be a “whys” person! Asking why is the beginning of scientific thinking.

1.2 Science Is Everywhere!

The most wonderful thing about science is that it is everywhere: from the ocean to outer space, from the kitchen to the playground, and from the rain outside to the phone in your hand.

🍳

Kitchen Science

Cooking uses heat, changes in materials, and chemical reactions.

🌦️

Weather & Rain

Evaporation, clouds, and rainfall make the water cycle visible.

🧲

Magnets at Home

Fridge doors, compasses, and magnetic toys all use magnetism.

💡

Electricity & Light

Bulbs, shadows, and reflections show physics in daily life.

Ideas in science are connected. Food, water, light, magnets, and stars are all parts of one big story.

1.3 The Scientific Method

Science is not just memorising facts. It is a step-by-step way of finding reliable answers. If your pen stops writing, you observe the problem, ask why, make a guess, test it, and analyse the result.

1
Observation
Notice something using your senses. Example: “My pen has stopped writing.”
2
Question
Ask why or how. Example: “Why did my pen stop writing?”
3
Hypothesis
Make an educated guess. Example: “Maybe the ink has finished.”
4
Experiment
Test the guess. Example: “Open the pen and check the refill.”
5
Analysis & Conclusion
Check what happened. If the guess is wrong, make a new hypothesis and test again.
In one line: Observe → Question → Hypothesize → Experiment → Analyse → Repeat if needed.

1.4 Thinking Like a Scientist

Scientists follow the scientific method to solve problems and discover new things. But anyone who follows this method is working like a scientist. You do not need a white lab coat!

👨‍🍳

The Cook

Observes dal spilling, guesses too much water, tests with less water.

🔧

Bicycle Repairer

Finds where air is leaking and tests possible causes.

🏥

Doctor

Observes symptoms, forms a diagnosis, treats, and checks results.

Failures are part of science. Thomas Edison’s many attempts before perfecting the light bulb show that wrong results still teach us something.
Science is rarely done alone. Scientists work in teams, repeat experiments, and verify each other’s results.

1.5 Science in Daily Life & Common Misconceptions

Learning science develops our ability to find solutions and solve mysteries. To learn science well, the first step is to be curious and observe keenly.

❌

Myth: Science is only in labs

Fact: Science is in kitchens, playgrounds, hospitals, farms, and homes.

❌

Myth: Science is only for geniuses

Fact: Science is for everyone who asks questions and tests ideas.

❌

Myth: Science is memorising

Fact: Science is a way of thinking, reasoning, experimenting, and concluding.

Reflection: Why is asking questions important in science?
Every scientific discovery starts with a question. Questions lead to hypotheses, hypotheses lead to experiments, and experiments lead to answers. Curiosity drives science forward.
